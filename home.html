About Me

What first sparked my interest toward technology was when my parents would purchase a new electrical appliance. From computers to surround-sound speaker systems, my parents would let me take over and explore the workings of each device. Throughout elementary school, I would apply this knowledge in my computer classes so by my junior-high graduation, I had received an Academic Excellence award in Computing. By the end of high school, I had obtained six credit hours for AP Java AB as well as an Academic Excellence award in Honors Java and an Honorable Mention ("iDesk") for the 2007 Toshiba/NSTA ExploraVision Awards Program. Today, I am the go-to guy for quick help to both friends and family. I recently helped two of my closest friends from high school who have served as IT specialists at JMU, GMU, and the Government Printing Office. Now I am finished with my BS in Computer Science at William and Mary.

What has helped me reach this point is my personal motivation. When my parents purchased my first computer, once again I quickly discovered all the many wonders one could do with it. One of these was a social network(formerly known as "Piczo") that allowed you to customize every aspect of the website you were given. While traveling along other websites finding out how I could customize my own, I soon learned the basics of the HTML language by seventh grade. Similarly, I have recently participated in two open source programs that force you to write your own code. With one, called Rainmeter, I created another layer to Windows that contains updates on my RSS feeds, music, weather, date, and time. On another, Autohotkey, I have reduced typing as much as possible by creating my own scripts to certain keystrokes. Both of these programs have their own scripting languages that I have learned on my leisure time.

However, this does not mean that I spend all of my free time on technology. I also enjoy spending time with my fellow peers. In high school, I performed in all the plays and musicals, including lead actor roles, and received multiple award nominations. In my senior year, I was elected Secretary of the Drama Club and asked to improve communication between its members and leaders. Therefore, I established an email database of all its members and sent important announcements to them every week. Finally, for my theatre program that meets over the summer, I co-created its website to provide information on performances and tickets.

Though I still am interested in theatre today, I am focusing more on technology. Coming into spring of my sophomore year, I had no knowledge of the languages C and C++ and the operating system Linux but was told that they were all required for my Algorithms and Systems Programming classes. Starting from scratch, I was forced to not only learn them within weeks but to write complicated programs with them. Despite working early on the projects, I would be writing hundreds of lines on the night before the deadline with fellow members of the CS department. At the end of it all, I received an A in Algorithms and a B in Systems Programming which included an online game of Nim over the internet via terminal. When it comes to these large problems, I would do everything in my power to get the job done, even if that includes viewing it from a different angle. While I got my BS in Computer Science from William and Mary, I double majored in Philosophy, improving my problem-solving and critical thinking abilities.

Well, aside from working a full year at Booz Allen Hamilton as a consultant, web developer and designer, that's where I am at this point in my life.
